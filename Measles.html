Measles Cases Rise Again

Measles cases are rising again in some countries, especially in Bangladesh and parts of the US. Health officials say lower vaccination rates after the pandemic may be one reason.

About Measles Cases Rise Again

Measles is a highly contagious disease caused by a virus. It spreads through coughing, sneezing, and close contact with infected people. Symptoms include fever, cough, red eyes, and a skin rash.

Why Are Cases Rising

Health experts say measles cases are rising again because fewer people are getting vaccinated in some countries. Increased travel and crowded places also help the virus spread faster.

How to Stay Safe

  • Get the measles vaccine on time
  • Wash your hands regularly
  • Avoid close contact with sick people
  • Cover your mouth when coughing or sneezing
  • Keep classrooms and homes clean
  • Visit a doctor if symptoms appear early
